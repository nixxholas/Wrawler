What are some ways to say "nice try" without it being misconstrued as condescending?
I was in a situation where I genuinely thought someone made a great effort and I wanted to encourage them even though they didn... More
I was in a situation where I genuinely thought someone made a great effort and I wanted to encourage them even though they didn't achieve the desired result. I wrote "nice try" (Note: This was in an online chatroom.) and got a very hostile and defensive response.

I did eventually clarify that I didn't mean it in a patronising manner, but is there a good, concise way to praise someone for their effort without any chance of being misunderstood?

Good effort.  (You can replace "good" with any other synonym like "awesome," "great," "fantastic," etc.)
You'll get it next time.
You did your best.  (which has already been said)
You did a great (et al) job anyway.
Awesome (et al) work anyway.
You're on the right track.
Regardless, I really admire your effort.

Anything to that effect will work.

"Nice try" actually is used with the intention of praising effort, but as you figured out, is often used as a put-down, normally said in a sarcastic tone.  I've used "nice try" in my classroom before when praising students for answering but not getting it right.  It's all in how you say it.

"Nice try" is usually said to children.  That's probably why you got the reaction you did. 

I don't think there is a particular phrase or couple of phrases you're supposed to use in this situation.  I can't think of any that couldn't necessarily be interpreted as dismissive or patronizing. 

It would probably be easiest to choose whatever aspect of that person's work was best in your view and praise it specifically.  That way, the person knows that you are aware of what they actually did and appreciate something other than the overall outcome.

In the situation where this occured, an online chatroom, people can't see or hear in what way you mean what you said. Using emoticons can help with this. In this specific case, adding a smiley to your statement can make a world of difference. You can't really interpret "nice try :)" in a hostile manner.

Don't overdo the use of emoticons though. Use it only with sentences that are susceptible to getting interpreted incorrectly.
