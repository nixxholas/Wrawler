5.0 out of 5 starsCashing Happy Checks
By Mark Eremite VINE VOICE on May 20, 2013
Format: MP3 Music Verified Purchase
He is tied with Jeff Garlin and T.J. Miller for the dubious honor of being the most obnoxious guest on Doug Benson's popular podcast, Doug Loves Movies. On that podcast, he's the kind of unbridled attention hog who sprays jokes out in a machine gun style so brassy that it's ten times funnier than anything he actually says. And he also habitually commits one of my biggest pet peeves for comedians: routinely laughing (long and loudly) at his own material.

Consider these complaints as an underscore to the following: this album is fantastic.

What a relief to find something so accessibly weird and warmly goofy and still legitimately funny! And I've been searching. I've been on a stand-up kick for the past few months, and the number of D.O.A. albums out there is just plain stifling. I enjoyed Pete's first album quite a bit, although there were bits that didn't stick so much, either because of their visual nature or their general done-to-death-ness (he's the third or fourth I've heard do entire sets on Subway sandwiches).

There's something about this album, though, that transcends any of the petty complaints I could level against it. The bits are tight without seeming rehearsed. Even the topics that seem overly done(breast milk, girlfriend friends, dying) are revived by his aggressively cheerful insistence. At one point, he claims he doesn't have the "poetry of language" to accurately describe an experience, but that -- if anything -- is where most of his humor lies: his ability to paint every moment with descriptions of colorful lunacy or to give them a self-effacing backdrop that falls just shy of being self-pitying and becomes more of a smirk and shrug and a "this is life" delivery.

Tons of fun. Surprising. Fast-paced. A treat. It's currently the #1 for this websites comedy offerings. There's little question why.
