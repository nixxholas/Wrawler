    "try-out" in British English

     See all translations

    try-outnoun [ C ]

    uk ​ /ˈtraɪ.aʊt/ us ​ /ˈtraɪ.aʊt/ informal

    › a test to see how useful or effective something or someone is:

    The try-outs for the team will be next weekend.
    After a try-out in Bath, the play is due to open in Edinburgh next month.

    "try out" in American English

     See all translations

    try out

    — phrasal verb with try us ​ /trɑɪ/ verb

    (COMPETE)

    › to compete for a position on the team or a part in the play:

    Jim tried out for the school play.
