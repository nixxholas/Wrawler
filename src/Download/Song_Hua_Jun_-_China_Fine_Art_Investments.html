Song Hua Jun

GanZhou (Jiang Xi Province) 1973

Song Hua Jun grew up in Shenzhen where everywhere is green all year round. He was inspired to paint using various shades of green expressing his ‘inner self’. Song Hua Jun uses his favourite colours to convey personality and emotional expression and green has become his means of expressing thoughts and feelings.

In recent years, Song Hua Jun’s art has been welcomed by the market at home and abroad. At auction, the transaction price of his work and the turnover rate is constantly being refreshed. He has huge potential and auctions and collections are very optimistic about his prospects; in particular his expressionistic style that China has long sought after.

Main Experience & Achievements:

1998 – Member of the Artists Association of China

1999 – ‘Lotus Flowers’ 8th Jiang Xi Art Exhibition

2001 – ‘Hometown Spring’ 6th Shenzhen Dafen Exhibition

2002 -  ‘My Garden’ 80th Anniversary of Jiang Xi Province Exhibition

2004 – China Prize of Contemporary Art

Song Hua Jun says:

I like the colour green, which is symbol of harmony, nature, peace, vitality and vigour. In my lotus series works, I hope you can feel that the vibrant green lotus leaves are enjoying sucking air and sunshine and showing their vitality; the flowers and leaves, absorbing the sun, seem to melt the air.

Lotus Pond 1: 75cm x 100cm Oil on Canvas - 2013

The elegance of the lotuses in a green and wide stretch of lotus pond; the viewer presented with a silent overflow from the fountain of life which never gets dry. This work is fresh and natural – you can ‘breathe in’ the painting, smell the flowers and the life of the blossoming lotus pond.

Lotus Pond 2: 80cm x 80cm Oil on Canvas - 2013

The elegance of the lotuses in a green and wide stretch of lotus pond is clear in this painting. The viewer is presented with a silent overflow from the fountain of life which never gets dry. This work looks fresh and natural – ‘breathe in’ the painting and feel the flowers and the life of the blossoming lotus pond.

Lotus Rhythm: 100cm  x 75cm Oil on Canvas - 2013

An eternal subject matter favoured by Song Hua Jun, the lotus is rich in vitality and beauty. This painting is created with bright green colours and possesses a unique sense of dimension. Large leaves and flowers stand straight and almost occupy the entire picture, suggesting a sense of indomitable spirit or perhaps representing another spirit: the unstained.

Spring by the River: 80cx5m x 80cm Oil on Canvas - 2013

A very simple work featuring a cluster of bright flowers growing amongst the dark stones along a stone bridge. The varying pink shades used to create the flowers and their casual embellishment creates a sense of deep floral fragrance. The density of composition is very interesting: some flowers tightly hug each other while others are more sparsely populated to create a sense of organic beauty. The artist skilfully fuses the flowers and the bridge into a cohesive whole to depict his love for spring.



Sunday Afternoon: 100cm x 75cm Oil on Canvas - 2013

Impressionistic in style - the artist captures the trees nuances of light and shadow to accentuate the unique feeling of space. It is about a family, father, mother and daughter, resting under the green trees on Sunday afternoon. Both the father and mother are looking at the daughter who carries the hope of the family. The work displays the ‘One Child Policy’ which brings people leisure, unwillingness and embarrassment.

Footbridge and River: 75cm x 100cm Oil on Canvas - 2013

The clear water in this work could be perceived as a crescent pond or perhaps a stream. ‘Footbridge and River’ reflects the mood of spring through the use of traditional green colour and modern western painting form. The countryside the artist has created is fresh and elegant, graceful and beautiful. The realistic approach is interspersed with impressionistic brushwork. It is full of rich exoticism and Chinese-style aesthetic rhyme.
