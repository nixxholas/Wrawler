Shuttlers Tan Wei Han, Terry Hee ready for SEA Games debut

Although the pair has enjoyed a string of successes, Terry Hee says they have to be wary of competition from traditional powerhouses Indonesia and Malaysia.  

  • By Nur Afifah Ariffin
  • Posted 24 Apr 2015 22:44
  • Updated 25 Apr 2015 00:53
Shuttlers Tan Wei Han (left) and Terry Hee (right). (Photo: Afifah Ariffin)
  • Enlarge
  • Caption

SINGAPORE: Badminton will return to the 28th Southeast Asian Games (SEA Games) after it was omitted in the Myanmar edition in 2013.

For a pair of Singapore players, the event is of even greater significance as they will be making their maiden appearance at the SEA Games. Shuttler Tan Wei Han has represented Singapore in numerous international competitions before, but according to her, the Games will be a very special one for her. 

"I think I am more excited because the SEA Games is on home ground in Singapore this year and it is my first time taking part, so I am really excited to take part in the competition."

While Tan is currently nursing a knee injury, she said she is confident that she will be ready by June. "Trainings have definitely been intensive. Our coaches are very focused on SEA Games players. But for me, I have an injury so I am doing mostly rehabilitation and training at the same time. All my coaches and strength trainers are doing their best to prep me for the Games. "

Tan will compete in the mixed doubles event with Terry Hee, who will also be making his first SEA Games appearance. The pair won the Singapore International Series in August last year and emerged as runners-up in the Malaysia International Challenge in November.

Although the pair has enjoyed a string of successes, Hee said they have to be wary of competition from traditional powerhouses Indonesia and Malaysia. "They are much higher ranking than us, and they have much more experience. But it is good to play against higher level players so we can learn better and hope to win."

The 20-year-old is looking forward to competing on home soil and hopes Singaporeans will come out to support them. "To play in Singapore, I think it is good that more supporters, more Singaporeans can help the team and myself a lot (by) cheering us on, and that will inspire us to do better," said Hee.

Badminton events will be played at the Singapore Indoor Stadium from Jun 10 to 16.
