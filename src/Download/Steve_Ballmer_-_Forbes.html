Steve Ballmer may have left his posts as Microsoft CEO and director in 2014, but he's made it clear that he's hanging on to his 4% stake. "I'd like to own Microsoft shares until I either give something to charity or I die," Ballmer told FORBES in 2014. He's now the company's largest individual shareholder, owning more stock than Microsoft cofounder Bill Gates, who has diversified his wealth over time. In October 2015, Ballmer announced that he'd also acquired a 4% stake in Twitter. Ballmer's main focus now is the Los Angeles Clippers, which he bought for $2 billion in August 2014, the highest price ever paid for an NBA team. Ballmer and his wife, Connie, have started on the philanthropic path, donating $11 million to the Washington State Opportunity Scholarship in September 2015 to support disadvantaged students pursuing degrees in the STEM fields or health care. In November 2014, they announced a $50 million gift to the University of Oregon and an undisclosed donation to Harvard's computer science department. A Detroit native, Ballmer dropped out of Stanford's MBA program to join Harvard classmate Bill Gates in 1980 as employee number 30. Ballmer took over for him as CEO in January 2000. More »
